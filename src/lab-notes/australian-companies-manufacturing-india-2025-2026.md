# Australian Companies Manufacturing in India: 2025-2026 Industry Analysis

*Published: February 8, 2026*

## Executive Summary

Australia-India manufacturing partnerships have reached unprecedented levels in 2025-2026, with **over 600 Australian companies** now operating in India across diverse sectors. This analysis examines the key players, manufacturing strategies, and industry trends driving this bilateral manufacturing boom.

## Key Findings

### Scale of Operations
- **600+ Australian companies** currently operating in India
- **60 companies** specifically in infrastructure manufacturing
- **1,000+ Australian companies** pursuing Indian manufacturing projects
- Manufacturing operations span **15+ major sectors**

### Trade Framework Benefits
The **Australia-India Cooperation and Trade Agreement (AI-ECTA)** has eliminated key barriers, enabling:
- Duty-free import of Australian components
- Simplified export procedures
- Enhanced bilateral manufacturing partnerships

## Sector-by-Sector Analysis

### 1. Food Equipment Manufacturing

#### **Heat and Control (Brisbane)**
**Established:** 2006 | **Location:** Chennai, Tamil Nadu  
**Employees:** 600+ manufacturing, sales, and administrative staff

**Operations:**
- Bespoke food-manufacturing equipment production
- 70% of production exported globally (South America, Europe, Southeast Asia)
- Specialized in both Western-style snacks and traditional Indian 'namkeen' processing
- Capacity: Up to 3 tonnes per hour production lines

**Strategic Advantage:**
- Australian engineering expertise combined with Indian manufacturing costs
- AI-ECTA framework enables seamless component imports and finished goods exports

### 2. Textile and Apparel Manufacturing

#### **Aussiebum**
**Status:** Setting up manufacturing by end of FY 2025  
**Target:** 5% of India's innerwear market by FY 2026

**Manufacturing Strategy:**
- Domestic Indian market focus with global export capability
- Over 1 million units annually (current global production)
- Revenue reinvestment model - profits stay in India for local manufacturing expansion

**Market Context:**
- Indian innerwear market: ₹48,123 crore (2025)
- Projected CAGR: 11.5%
- Market value target: ₹26,952 crore by 2025

### 3. Mining and Resources

#### **BHP**
**Role:** Commodity supplier to Indian manufacturing  
**Focus:** Steel production, copper needs, infrastructure materials

**Current Status (2025-2026):**
- India emerging as key demand engine
- Strong domestic steel activity sustaining demand
- Copper requirements rising with manufacturing expansion
- Supporting India's 300Mt steel capacity target by 2030

#### **Rio Tinto**
**Operations:** Iron ore partnerships and mining equipment supply  
**Strategic Position:** Long-term supplier to Indian steel manufacturers

### 4. Infrastructure and Construction

**Active Companies:**
- **SMEC Holdings** - Engineering and infrastructure consulting
- **Macquarie Group** - Financial infrastructure and investment
- **CIMIC Group** - Construction and mining services

### 5. Technology and Manufacturing

#### **Emerging Sectors:**
- **AI and Machine Learning:** Appen, SenSen Networks
- **Telecommunications:** Telstra Global Capability Centers (Bengaluru, Pune, Hyderabad)
- **5G and IoT Solutions:** Australian tech firms expanding manufacturing partnerships

## Manufacturing Strategies and Trends

### 1. **Export-Oriented Manufacturing**
Companies leverage India's cost advantages for:
- Local component sourcing
- Manufacturing for global markets
- Supply chain diversification

### 2. **Joint Venture Models**
- Australian technical expertise + Indian manufacturing capacity
- Local partnership requirements for market access
- Technology transfer agreements

### 3. **Domestic Market Focus**
- India's growing middle class driving domestic demand
- Local production for import substitution
- Government 'Make in India' initiative alignment

### 4. **Supply Chain Integration**
- Australian components → Indian assembly → Global exports
- Regional supply chain optimization
- Risk diversification strategies

## Industry Challenges and Opportunities

### Challenges
- **Regulatory Complexity:** Bureaucratic processes and approval delays
- **Quality Standards:** Meeting Australian and international quality requirements
- **Labor Relations:** Managing cross-cultural manufacturing operations
- **Infrastructure:** Supporting large-scale manufacturing operations

### Opportunities
- **Market Size:** India's $535 billion food processing sector by 2025-26
- **Cost Advantages:** 60-70% cost reduction vs. Australian manufacturing
- **Government Support:** AI-ECTA benefits and 'Make in India' incentives
- **Skilled Workforce:** Engineering and technical expertise availability

## Future Outlook (2026-2028)

### Expected Growth Areas
1. **Food Processing Equipment:** Continued expansion driven by automation needs
2. **Electronics Manufacturing:** Australian tech companies establishing production
3. **Renewable Energy Equipment:** Solar and wind manufacturing partnerships
4. **Healthcare Manufacturing:** Medical devices and pharmaceutical equipment

### Projected Metrics
- **15% increase** in Australian manufacturing partnerships by 2027
- **$2.5 billion** bilateral manufacturing trade volume target
- **New manufacturing hubs** in Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Maharashtra

## Conclusion

Australian manufacturing in India represents a **strategic shift** from cost arbitrage to **value-added partnerships**. Companies are not merely outsourcing production but building **integrated manufacturing ecosystems** that leverage Australian innovation and Indian scale.

The success of partnerships like Heat and Control demonstrates that **manufacturing excellence** rather than just **cost reduction** is driving the current boom. As AI-ECTA benefits fully materialize and India's manufacturing infrastructure matures, Australian companies are positioned to play an increasingly important role in India's industrial transformation.
